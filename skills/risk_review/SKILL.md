# risk_review

Purpose: Prevent common civic-tech mistakes and enforce guardrails.

## When To Use
- Before sharing claims publicly, shipping copy, or demoing.
- When consent, privacy, representational bias, or legal claims appear.
- When a tool claims to aggregate "all" stories or "all" events.

## Inputs
- Product copy, UI text, README/marketing claims, demo script.
- Research outputs and cited sources.

## Outputs
- List of flagged risks: hallucinated services, consent/privacy gaps, representational bias claims, missing/weak sources, misleading language.
- Concrete fixes: rewordings, source additions, scope trims, required disclaimers.
- Any open questions to resolve with officials or partner institutions.

## Process
1) Check all civic claims for official sources; require links.
2) Remove/soften representational claims ("all Richmond voices", "what Richmond thinks"); require explicit limitation language.
3) Verify consent mechanisms exist for any story submission feature.
4) Check attribution for all aggregated arts event content — source organizations must be credited.
5) Verify Richmond specificity; avoid generalizing across jurisdictions.
6) Ensure weekend‑feasible scope; cut features that require new policy or sensitive data.
7) Summarize fixes and request confirmation to apply changes.
