# Continuation Plan — A City That Tells Its Stories

Post-hackathon continuation opportunities, potential partners, and next steps. This pillar has more natural champions than any other pillar in the hackathon.

---

## Potential Champions

- Role: Director/founder of the Shockoe Institute, which focuses on Richmond history and storytelling
- Relevance: Strong alignment with neighborhood history and civic storytelling themes
- Why this matters: A champion who can advocate for continued use of a story collection or neighborhood history tool within the institutional ecosystem

### Meg Hughes — The Valentine (Richmond History Center)
- Role: Staff at The Valentine, Richmond's history museum with extensive oral history collections

### Sylvio Lynch III — Richmond History & Culture Commission
- Role: Member of the Richmond History & Culture Commission
- Relevance: City-connected champion who can advocate for civic use of story collection tools

### Chaya Braxton — Office of Strategic Communications, City of Richmond
- Role: City communications staff
- Relevance: The Office of Strategic Communications would be the natural City home for an arts event aggregator or public-facing story tool
- Why this matters: City adoption of an arts aggregator or story portal requires a communications champion

## Continuation Pathways by Project Type

### Arts Event Aggregator (Concepts 1 and 2)
- **30 days:** Confirm Eventbrite API coverage of Richmond arts events; identify 10-15 additional RSS/iCal sources from arts organizations; publish beta
- **60 days:** Approach the Office of Strategic Communications (Chaya Braxton) about formal endorsement or integration into richmond.gov
- **90 days:** Evaluate partnership with Visit Richmond or Richmond Region Tourism for broader cultural tourism alignment
- **Sustainability model:** Low-maintenance aggregator can run with minimal hosting costs if sources maintain their own feeds; City or arts organization could absorb minimal operating costs
- **Key risk:** Without an institutional home, the aggregator may have no one to maintain source list or handle broken feeds

### Story Collection Portal (Concept 4)
- **30 days:** Partner with The Valentine (Meg Hughes) to review consent language and archive approach; recruit 20 beta storytellers from Church Hill or another focus neighborhood
- **60 days:** Approach Richmond History & Culture Commission (Sylvio Lynch III) about formal connection to City civic engagement processes
- **90 days:** Evaluate integration with existing City engagement platforms (participatory budgeting, planning comment processes)
- **Sustainability model:** Requires institutional home for story storage and use — The Valentine, VCU Libraries, or a City department
- **Key risk:** Stories collected without a committed use pathway will feel like archiving to contributors

### Neighborhood History Explorer (Concept 6)
- **30 days:** Formalize content partnership with The Valentine for seed content; identify 3-5 additional stories to add per week from museum collections or community submissions
- **60 days:** Evaluate QR code installation in pilot neighborhood (Jackson Ward or Church Hill) with City of Richmond and neighborhood association blessing
- **90 days:** Seek grant funding through National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH Digital Humanities grants) or Virginia Humanities for sustained development
- **Sustainability model:** Museum partnership is the natural home; VCU Digital Humanities could provide technical support
- **Key risk:** Seed content quality determines whether the tool is compelling enough to attract continued use and submission
