# Quickstart

## Fastest path for teams
1. Read `00_core/00_pillar_overview.md`
2. Read `01_problem_space/02_targeted_problem_statements.md`
3. Read `02_data/00_index.md`
4. Pick one user and one problem
5. Read `04_build_guides/01_mvp_shapes.md`
6. Create your project brief using `99_templates/project_one_pager_template.md`

## Questions to answer before building
- Who is the user (Richmond resident seeking events, longtime neighborhood resident with stories, arts organization staff, City communications staff)?
- What problem are they facing?
- What data or documents are you using?
- What can actually be built in a weekend?
- How will a judge understand the value in under 60–90 seconds?

## High-probability project types
- arts event aggregator (pulling from Eventbrite, RSS, gallery calendars)
- "What's On in RVA" discovery feed filtered by date, neighborhood, or type
- arts and culture district map with links to event sources
- resident story collection portal with guided prompts and consent flow
- neighborhood history explorer connecting place to people and memory

## This pillar's special character
This pillar has the **highest accessibility scores** across all seven pillars (D8 = 4 for both targeted statements). That means the best ideas here serve residents who are **not** already comfortable with formal civic processes — older residents, non-digital users, people in rapidly changing neighborhoods.

The **highest-scoring blue sky statement** across all pillars lives here: "Exploring Richmond Through Its Stories" scored 24/27 (Exceptional). Let that ambition inform your work, but keep your MVP weekend-feasible.

## What to avoid
- A manually-curated comprehensive city events calendar (will decay without sustained staff)
- Claiming your story tool represents all Richmond voices (it won't — be honest about who participates)
- Duplicating oral history work by The Valentine or Black History Museum without a partnership plan
- Requiring official City endorsement of story content
