# F5 Opportunities Do Not Build: Strategic Priorities 2025-2026

*Why F5 is consciously stepping back from select product categories and what that means for partners, investors, and customers.*

## Executive Summary

F5 Inc. has accelerated its transition from hardware-centric load-balancing to a software-first, distributed-cloud model. The company has publicly announced the discontinuation of BIG-IP Next hardware and virtual edition (VE) systems as of August 2025 [1]. Furthermore, F5 has made strategic decisions not to build its own standalone Content Delivery Network (CDN), Security Information and Event Management (SIEM), or Extended Detection and Response (XDR) solutions. 

These decisions are driven by a revenue mix shift toward software subscriptions, which reached $164 million in Q1 FY 2026 [2], and a strategic focus on AI-enabled Distributed Cloud Services (DCS) [3]. Customer demand for hybrid-multicloud flexibility is also a major factor, with 94% of organizations now deploying applications across multiple environments [4]. The "do-not-build" stance reduces capital-intensive R&D, protects margins, and aligns F5 with market expectations for programmable, AI-driven infrastructure.

## 1. Strategic Shift from Hardware to Software

F5's financial results demonstrate a clear pivot toward software and services. In Q1 2026, the company reported total revenue of $822 million, representing a 7% year-over-year increase [5]. 

| Metric | Q1 FY 2026 Performance | Trend / Context |
|--------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **Total Revenue** | $822 million | Up 7% year-over-year, reflecting a balanced mix of products and services [5]. |
| **Product Revenue** | $410 million | Up 11% year-over-year [5]. |
| **Software Revenue** | $192 million | Declined 8% year-over-year overall, but subscription software grew [2]. |
| **Subscription Software** | $164 million | Up 1% year-over-year, offsetting declines in perpetual licenses ($27 million) [2]. |

*The financial data highlights F5's ongoing transition. While legacy software models (perpetual) decline, the core focus remains on stabilizing and growing the subscription base.*

By de-emphasizing new hardware development, F5 can allocate R&D dollars to SaaS and AI, preserving high-margin subscription growth and adapting to a market where 79% of organizations are repatriating apps to optimize costs and security across hybrid environments [4].

## 2. Discontinued Products and "Do Not Build" Categories

### Discontinuation of BIG-IP Next Hardware & VE
In August 2025, F5 made the strategic decision to discontinue the development of BIG-IP Next hardware and VE (Virtual Edition) systems [1]. This move forces a shift to cloud-native, software-defined delivery, as customers increasingly ask for agility and lower total cost of ownership. Existing customers are guided toward migration paths to BIG-IP Next-Gen (software) or Distributed Cloud Services.

### Products Explicitly Not Built
Rather than competing directly with hyperscalers or specialized security vendors in commoditized spaces, F5 has opted out of building certain standalone platforms.

| Category | Strategic Stance & Reasoning |
|----------|------------------------------|
| **CDN (Content Delivery Network)** | F5 will not build a standalone CDN. The market is dominated by hyperscalers. Instead, F5 focuses on API security, edge connectivity, and native CDN caching visibility within its Distributed Cloud Services [3]. |
| **SIEM (Security Information & Event Management)** | Not in the roadmap. F5 prefers a partnership model and relies on AI-driven security analytics integrated directly into its existing platforms. |
| **XDR (Extended Detection & Response)** | F5 is not pursuing XDR as a standalone product. The concentration remains on application-level security (WAAP) and AI Guardrails rather than broad-scope endpoint and network detection. |

*These exclusions allow F5 to focus strictly on application delivery and security, avoiding areas where it lacks a distinct competitive advantage.*

## 3. Financial Impact of the "Do-Not-Build" Policy

The decision to avoid building CDN, SIEM, and XDR platforms has a direct impact on F5's financial outlook and resource allocation. 

Following strong Q1 2026 results, F5 raised its full-year 2026 revenue growth outlook to 5%-6%, up from a prior outlook of 0%-4% [6]. The company also expects mid-single-digit software revenue growth for the year [6]. By avoiding the massive capital expenditures required to build a global CDN or a comprehensive SIEM from scratch, F5 protects its gross margins and redirects investments toward high-yield AI collaborations, such as its expanded strategic collaboration with NetApp to enhance AI application performance [7].

## 4. AI-Enabled Distributed Cloud Services (7.0 Release)

Instead of building disparate products, F5 is consolidating capabilities into its Application Delivery and Security Platform (ADSP). The December 2025 release of F5 Distributed Cloud Services 7.0 serves as a strategic milestone in this journey [3].

Key enhancements in the 7.0 release that replace the need for separate CDN/SIEM/XDR include:
* **API Discovery & Security:** Extends API discovery and security for applications deployed outside the console, including those supported by F5 BIG-IP, F5 NGINX, and third-party gateways [3].
* **Granular Bot Defense:** Improved controls and tighter integration with managed F5 Distributed Cloud Bot Defense services, giving customers greater self-serviceability [3].
* **CDN Cacheable Content Tool:** A new view for HTTP load balancers that shows how much content can be cached and delivered with native CDN services, helping customers fine-tune app delivery without requiring a separate CDN product [3].
* **Platform Resilience & Observability:** Enhanced logging, real-time service status, and new diagnostic tooling provide deep observability, fueling AI-driven insights [3].

## 5. Partner & Customer Recommendations

| Audience | Recommended Action | Strategic Rationale |
|----------|--------------------|---------------------|
| **Existing Hardware Customers** | Initiate migration planning from BIG-IP Next hardware/VE to Distributed Cloud Services or software alternatives. | F5 has officially discontinued BIG-IP Next hardware/VE development [1]. Early migration preserves support and unlocks AI features. |
| **Channel Partners** | Upskill on DCS API security, bot-defense, and AI integrations. | Align with F5's go-to-market focus on SaaS and ADSP adoption [3], capturing higher-margin recurring revenue. |
| **Investors** | Track software subscription growth and FY 2026 revenue targets (5%-6% growth) [6]. | Hardware de-investment reduces capital intensity; SaaS and software subscriptions ($164M in Q1 2026) drive long-term value [2]. |
| **IT Operations Leaders** | Focus on simplification and standardization of operations, including streamlining APIs. | 58% of organizations report APIs have become a pain point; AI systems are well-suited to handle this complexity autonomously [4]. |

*Stakeholders must align their strategies with F5's software-defined, AI-orchestrated future to maximize the value of their investments.*

## 6. Risks & Mitigation

| Risk Factor | Description | Mitigation Strategy |
|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Competitive Encroachment** | Hyperscalers offering bundled CDN/SIEM/XDR could lure price-sensitive customers away from F5's specialized ADSP. | Deepen integration partnerships (e.g., NetApp collaboration [7]) and emphasize the unique value of F5's AI-driven programmable security across hybrid environments. |
| **Customer Perception Gaps** | Customers may fear a loss of on-premises reliability following the discontinuation of BIG-IP Next hardware [1]. | Offer robust migration tools, emphasize hybrid-cloud support (as 94% of orgs use multiple environments [4]), and ensure clear communication on software capabilities. |
| **AI Operational Readiness** | 60% of organizations feel bogged down by manual workflows, and 54% claim skill shortages are barriers to AI development [4]. | F5 must ensure its DCS platform simplifies operations through natural language interfaces and automated policy deployment, reducing the burden on IT staff. |

*Proactive management of these risks is essential to ensure the success of F5's software-centric strategy through 2026 and beyond.*
