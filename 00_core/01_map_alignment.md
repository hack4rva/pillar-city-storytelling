# MAP Alignment — A City That Tells Its Stories

## Connection to the Richmond MAP Framework

The A City That Tells Its Stories pillar connects to the MAP (Moving Across Pillars) framework through several civic threads:

### Civic Identity and Cultural Vibrancy
Richmond has a distinct cultural identity built from its history, its arts community, and the diverse neighborhoods that make up the city. When residents cannot find or access that cultural life, civic identity weakens. Arts discovery tools strengthen the connective tissue between residents and their city's creative economy.

### Inclusive Representation
The MAP framework commits Richmond to governance that includes all residents — not just those already comfortable with formal civic processes. The storytelling problem is fundamentally a representation problem: current engagement tools (surveys, public comment, 311) capture structured feedback from civically-active residents. They do not capture the lived experience of long-time residents, particularly from underrepresented communities, in ways that inform planning and policy.

### Place-Based Storytelling
Richmond is a city of distinct neighborhoods with distinct histories. The MAP framework recognizes place as central to identity and community. Tools that connect resident narratives to specific places — Church Hill, Jackson Ward, Northside, Fulton — make the civic value of storytelling concrete and locatable.

### Cultural Infrastructure
Arts organizations, galleries, community spaces, and cultural institutions are civic infrastructure. When they are invisible to residents, that infrastructure is underutilized. Aggregation and discovery tools are lightweight civic investments that multiply the value of existing cultural infrastructure without requiring new spending.

### Time-Sensitivity of Community Memory
Richmond is experiencing rapid neighborhood change. The window to capture stories from long-time residents in changing neighborhoods — Fulton, Manchester, the Fan, parts of the Northside — is narrowing. The MAP framework's commitment to preservation and equity makes this pillar's urgency a MAP-level concern.
