Prior Art E3 — Place-Based Storytelling Tools

Research tools that connect stories to specific geographic places, particularly neighborhood-level storytelling tools. Include:

- Historypin: how it works, who uses it, technical implementation, strengths and weaknesses
- QR code neighborhood history installations: examples from other cities, implementation requirements
- Walking tour apps with narrative content (Detour, Soundwalk, similar)
- Museum map-based storytelling tools
- Richmond-specific: any place-based storytelling tools or neighborhood history apps currently in use in Richmond

For each, assess:
- Technical complexity to build in a weekend
- How seed content was obtained and maintained
- User engagement patterns
- Whether institutional partnerships were required

Output
- Inventory of place-based storytelling tools with technical complexity assessment
- Which models are weekend-buildable vs. which require more infrastructure
- Recommendation for a Richmond neighborhood history tool MVP shape
- Seed content requirements and realistic sources for Richmond
