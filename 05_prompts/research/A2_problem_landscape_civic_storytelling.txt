Problem Landscape A2 — Civic Storytelling Reality

Investigate why resident stories in Richmond are not currently captured or connected to City decisions. Using verifiable public information and clearly labeled inferences, explain: what civic engagement tools Richmond currently uses (surveys, 311, public comment), what they do and do not capture, and why lived experience narratives from longtime residents fall outside these systems.

Focus on Richmond, Virginia. Include what is known about neighborhood change dynamics in Richmond and why this makes story capture time-sensitive.

Output
- Facts (with source titles) about current Richmond civic engagement mechanisms
- Inferences (clearly labeled) about what those mechanisms miss
- Unknowns about the gap between story and governance in Richmond
- Assessment of why the "collection without use is archiving" risk is real here
- What Richmond-specific urgency exists (neighborhood change, aging residents, etc.)
