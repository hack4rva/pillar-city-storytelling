Risks G2 — Consent and Privacy for Storytellers

Investigate consent and privacy requirements for a civic story collection tool. Focus on:

- What constitutes valid informed consent for a resident sharing a personal story with a City-connected tool?
- What must a consent statement include: who sees the story, how it is stored, how it is used, how long it is kept, whether it may be published, right to withdraw
- Special considerations for sensitive stories (stories about displacement, discrimination, family hardship, criminal justice)
- Privacy risks specific to neighborhood history stories (identifying living individuals, revealing locations of people who may prefer not to be found)
- COPPA considerations if any minors might submit stories
- What data minimization looks like: what is the minimum personal information needed?

Output
- Required elements of a consent statement for this use case
- Special handling requirements for sensitive story content
- Data minimization guidelines (what to collect and what not to collect)
- Template consent language in plain English
- Template consent language in Spanish
- Recommendation for where stories should be stored and who should have access
