Users B3 — The Longtime Resident with Neighborhood Stories

Build a detailed persona for a longtime Richmond resident who holds valuable neighborhood history and lived experience but currently has no trusted, low-barrier channel to share it. The core example from the rubric is a 75-year-old African American woman from Church Hill who has watched her neighborhood change dramatically over six decades. Include:

- Demographic context and relationship to neighborhood
- Specific knowledge and stories she holds (types of memory: businesses, gatherings, social networks, institutions)
- Current barriers to sharing: technology access, institutional gatekeeping, uncertainty about whether her stories matter
- Preferred channels for communication: voice, phone, in-person, written
- Language considerations: English, Spanish, other languages present in Richmond communities
- Trust factors: what would make her trust that her stories will be handled respectfully

Base on evidence from comparable populations in Richmond and similar cities. Label inferences clearly.

Output
- Detailed persona with barriers, preferences, and motivations
- Specific design requirements for a tool to reach this user
- What makes this user the priority target (equity, urgency, irreplaceability of knowledge)
- What features would fail this user and why
