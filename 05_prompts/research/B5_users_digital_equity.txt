Users B5 — Digital Equity: Residents Who Use Voice, Phone, or Non-Web Channels

Investigate what is known about digital access and technology comfort levels in Richmond, Virginia, with particular attention to populations relevant to this pillar: elderly residents, residents in rapidly changing neighborhoods, residents with limited English proficiency, and low-income residents.

Include:
- Richmond-specific data on broadband access and smartphone ownership by demographic
- What is known about technology comfort levels for older Richmond residents
- What Richmond civic engagement tools currently exist for non-digital users
- What voice-based or phone-based civic engagement approaches have worked elsewhere
- Design patterns that make civic tech tools accessible to low-digital users

Output
- Richmond-specific evidence on digital equity (labeled Verified, Inference, Unknown)
- Specific accessibility requirements implied by the target population for this pillar
- Voice and phone design patterns that should be incorporated
- What a "digital equity" version of an arts discovery or story collection tool looks like
