Opportunity Spaces F4 — Where Software Helps and Where It Does Not

Analyze where technology genuinely helps in this pillar vs. where the problem requires human relationships, institutional commitment, or community trust that software cannot provide.

For each opportunity space, distinguish:
- What software can do (aggregation, discovery, guided collection, theme surfacing, geographic display)
- What software cannot do (building trust with skeptical residents, ensuring stories are acted on, maintaining institutional commitment to using stories, replacing trained oral historians and archivists)

Specifically address:
- What is the right role for AI/LLM in this pillar (theme extraction, prompt generation, translation) vs. where AI overclaims
- What must be human-led for the tool to be trustworthy and effective
- Where a weekend prototype is genuinely valuable vs. where it overpromises

Output
- Clear delineation of software roles vs. human roles for each opportunity
- Specific AI use cases that are appropriate and bounded
- Specific AI use cases that are inappropriate or overclaiming for this pillar
- Recommendation for how to frame the tool's role honestly in the demo
