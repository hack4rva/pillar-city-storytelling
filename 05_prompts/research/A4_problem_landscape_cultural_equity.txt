Problem Landscape A4 — Cultural Equity: Whose Stories Get Told

Investigate the dominant narrative problem in civic storytelling and cultural visibility tools. In Richmond, Virginia specifically, whose stories and cultural events are most visible, and whose are systematically less visible?

Consider:
- Which Richmond neighborhoods and communities are underrepresented in mainstream arts discovery channels
- Which resident populations are most likely to have their stories excluded from digital story collection tools
- Historical patterns of erasure in Richmond's civic and cultural narrative (Jackson Ward, Fulton, Church Hill, etc.)
- What design choices in past civic tech tools have amplified dominant narratives

Output
- Richmond-specific analysis of cultural visibility gaps (labeled as Verified, Inference, or Unknown)
- Design principles that make story collection and arts discovery tools more equitable
- Specific features that would help this pillar serve underrepresented communities
- Risks of a hackathon tool inadvertently making equity gaps worse
