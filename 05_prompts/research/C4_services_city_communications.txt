Services C4 — City of Richmond Communications Infrastructure

Investigate how the City of Richmond currently communicates about arts, culture, and community engagement. Include:

- Office of Strategic Communications: what it does, how it distributes arts and cultural information
- City of Richmond website (rva.gov): what arts and culture sections exist and what they contain
- Richmond's current civic engagement tools: participatory budgeting platforms, resident feedback portals, 311
- How the City currently promotes arts events vs. how it could
- City departments with relevant interest: Parks and Recreation (manages some cultural facilities), Planning (neighborhood engagement), Communications

Also investigate Chaya Braxton (Office of Strategic Communications) as a named potential champion for this pillar.

Output
- Inventory of City communications channels relevant to arts and culture
- Assessment of current gaps in City-facilitated arts discovery
- Assessment of current gaps in City-facilitated civic storytelling
- What the Office of Strategic Communications would need to formally adopt a hackathon prototype
- Recommendation on which City channel is the most natural home for an arts aggregator or story portal
